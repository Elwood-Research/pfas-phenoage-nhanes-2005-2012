## Detailed Reference Notes

### I. PFAS and Epigenetic Aging

#### Khodasevich et al. 2025 ⭐ KEY STUDY
**Title:** Sex-specific associations between per- and polyfluoroalkyl substance exposure and epigenetic age  
**Journal:** Environmental Research  
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.envres.2025.121827  
**Population:** NHANES 1999-2000 (N=262)  
**PFAS Measured:** 11 compounds (EPICv1 array)  
**Epigenetic Clocks:** 7 clocks including PhenoAge  
**Key Finding:** Sex-specific associations between PFAS and epigenetic age acceleration  
**Relevance:** Direct evidence in US population; establishes foundation for current study

#### Niemiec et al. 2023
**Title:** Prenatal exposures to per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances and epigenetic aging in umbilical cord blood  
**Journal:** Environmental Research  
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.envres.2023.116215  
**Study:** Healthy Start cohort  
**Finding:** Prenatal PFAS exposure associated with epigenetic age acceleration (EAA)  
**Implication:** Developmental origins of aging; PFAS effects may begin in utero

#### Xu et al. 2022
**Title:** Perfluoroalkyl substances influence DNA methylation in school-age children  
**Journal:** Environmental Epigenetics  
**DOI:** 10.1093/eep/dvac004  
**Population:** Ronneby, Sweden cohort (children 7-11 years)  
**Exposure:** High PFAS from contaminated drinking water (firefighting foam)  
**Key Finding:** PFAS exposure associated with altered DNA methylation and epigenetic age acceleration  
**Relevance:** High-exposure population demonstrating dose-dependent effects

#### Xu et al. 2020
**Title:** Associations between serum concentrations of perfluoroalkyl substances and DNA methylation in women  
**Journal:** Environment International  
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.envint.2020.106148  
**Population:** Women in Ronneby, Sweden  
**Innovation:** First study evaluating PFAS and epigenetic age acceleration in adults  
**Finding:** PFAS associated with altered methylation patterns  
**Gap Filled:** Adult women, not just children or prenatal

#### Conner et al. 2025
**Title:** Interventions to Reduce Serum PFAS Levels and Improve Epigenetic Age Acceleration in US Firefighters  
**Journal:** JMIR Research Protocols  
**DOI:** 10.2196/67120  
**Design:** Randomized controlled trial protocol  
**Intervention:** Blood/plasma donation to reduce PFAS  
**Outcomes:** PFAS levels, cardiovascular risk, epigenetic age acceleration  
**Importance:** First RCT testing reversibility of PFAS-induced aging

#### Fan et al. 2025
**Title:** Accelerated placental aging mediated the association between prenatal PFAS exposure and small-for-gestational age newborns  
**Journal:** Ecotoxicology and Environmental Safety  
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.ecoenv.2025.118338  
**Sample:** 1046 maternal-infant dyads  
**Key Finding:** Placental telomere length shortened with PFAS; mediated SGA risk  
**Innovation:** Demonstrated tissue-specific aging (placenta) and clinical consequence (SGA)

### II. PhenoAge Development and Validation

#### Levine et al. 2018 ⭐ FOUNDATIONAL
**Title:** An epigenetic biomarker of aging for lifespan and healthspan  
**Journal:** Aging  
**DOI:** 10.18632/aging.101414  
**Authors:** Morgan Levine, Steve Horvath, et al.  
**Citation Count:** 2638+  
**Innovation:** Developed DNAm PhenoAge integrating phenotypic age with DNA methylation  
**Components:** 9 clinical biomarkers + chronological age → phenotypic age → 513 CpGs → DNAm PhenoAge  
**Validation:** Outperformed previous clocks for mortality, cancer, healthspan, Alzheimer's  
**Pathways:** Pro-inflammatory, interferon, decreased transcription/translation, mitochondrial signatures  
**Importance:** Gold standard biological aging biomarker

#### Kuo et al. 2021
**Title:** Biological Aging Predicts Vulnerability to COVID-19 Severity in UK Biobank Participants  
**Journal:** Journals of Gerontology: Series A  
**DOI:** 10.1093/gerona/glab060  
**Sample:** 613 UK Biobank participants (2006-2010 baseline, 2020 COVID outcomes)  
**Design:** PhenoAge measured >10 years before pandemic  
**Key Finding:** PhenoAge predicted COVID-19 inpatient positivity and mortality (OR=1.63 per 5 years)  
**Implication:** PhenoAge captures long-term vulnerability before disease manifestation

#### Ho et al. 2023
**Title:** Biological age is superior to chronological age in predicting hospital mortality of the critically ill  
**Journal:** Internal and Emergency Medicine  
**DOI:** 10.1007/s11739-023-03397-3  
**Population:** 2950 ICU patients, Western Australia  
**Method:** Levine PhenoAge model  
**Finding:** PhenoAge and residuals better discriminated mortality than chronological age (AUROC 0.648, 0.654 vs 0.547)  
**Key Result:** PhenoAgeAccel HR=1.997 for mortality; dose-related fashion  
**Clinical Utility:** Applicable to acute care settings

#### Wang et al. 2025
**Title:** Aging measures and cancer in the Health and Retirement Study (HRS)  
**Journal:** Nature Communications  
**DOI:** 10.1038/s41467-025-60913-z  
**Sample:** 946 cancer survivors, 4555 controls (HRS)  
**Methods:** Physiology-based (KDM, PhenoAge) and epigenetic clocks (Horvath, Hannum, Levine, GrimAge, Zhang, mPOA)  
**Cancer Association:** Hannum, GrimAge, subjective age associated with cancer prevalence  
**Mortality Prediction:** GrimAge strongest in survivors (HR=1.80 per SD); PhenoAge significant in both groups  
**Implication:** Multiple aging measures have distinct predictive utilities

#### Anthony et al. 2025
**Title:** Biological age and clinical frailty scale measured at intensive care unit admission as predictors of hospital mortality  
**Journal:** Acute and Critical Care  
**DOI:** 10.4266/acc.000200  
**Sample:** 1073 ICU patients, Western Australia  
**Design:** Retrospective cohort  
**Finding:** PhenoAge and frailty independently predicted mortality; interaction between them  
**Clinical Application:** PhenoAge particularly predictive in non-frail patients  
**Innovation:** Demonstrated complementary value of biological age and frailty assessment

### III. PFAS and Cardiometabolic Health (NHANES)

#### Bhagavathula et al. 2026 ⭐ RECENT NHANES
**Title:** Association Between PFAS Exposure and LDL Cholesterol in US Adults: Evidence From NHANES  
**Journal:** Lipids  
**DOI:** 10.1002/lipd.70008  
**Sample:** 998 adults from NHANES 2017-2020 (representing 240 million US adults)  
**PFAS Measured:** 9 compounds (PFOA, PFOS, PFUnDA, PFHxS, etc.)  
**Method:** Double/debiased machine learning  
**Key Finding:** Total PFAS associated with higher LDL-C (β=0.30, p=0.016)  
**Specific Compounds:** PFUnDA, linear PFOS, Sm-PFOS significantly associated with elevated LDL-C  
**Mechanism:** Lipid dysregulation pathway  
**Relevance:** Recent US data; mechanistic link to PhenoAge (lipids → CVD → aging)

#### Bashir et al. 2022 ⭐ NHANES ALLOSTATIC LOAD
**Title:** The Association between Multiple PFAS Serum Levels and Allostatic Load  
**Journal:** Int J Environ Res Public Health  
**DOI:** 10.3390/ijerph19095455  
**Sample:** NHANES 2007-2014, adults 20+  
**PFAS:** PFHS, PFDE, PFBS, PFHP, PFDO, PFNA, PFUA, PFOA, PFOS  
**Allostatic Load:** Composite of 10 biomarkers (cardiovascular, inflammatory, metabolic)  
**Key Finding:** Positive association between most PFAS and allostatic load (adjusted)  
**Multicollinearity:** PFNA, PFOS, PFOA highly correlated (VIF >5); analyzed individually  
**Relevance:** Allostatic load conceptually similar to biological aging burden

#### Wu et al. 2023 - NHANES NAFLD
**Title:** PFAS exposure and risk of nonalcoholic fatty liver disease in the elderly: results from NHANES 2003-2014  
**Journal:** Environmental Science and Pollution Research  
**DOI:** 10.1007/s11356-023-26941-2  
**Population:** Elderly participants, NHANES 2003-2014  
**Outcome:** NAFLD prevalence  
**Finding:** PFAS exposure associated with increased NAFLD risk  
**Mechanism:** Hepatotoxicity, metabolic disruption  
**PhenoAge Link:** Liver function (albumin, alkaline phosphatase) is PhenoAge component

#### Zong et al. 2024
**Title:** Exposure to PFAS is associated with impaired cardiovascular health: a cross-sectional study  
**Journal:** Frontiers in Public Health  
**DOI:** 10.3389/fpubh.2024.1418134  
**Design:** Cross-sectional analysis of US adults  
**Outcome:** Cardiovascular health scores  
**Finding:** PFAS exposure associated with impaired CVH  
**Implication:** Cardiovascular pathway is major mechanism for PFAS-aging link

#### Liao et al. 2023 - NHANES INTERVENTION
**Title:** Association between probiotic consumption and serum PFAS: results from NHANES, 2003-2018  
**Journal:** Environmental Sciences Europe  
**DOI:** 10.1186/s12302-023-00808-2  
**Sample:** 9469 adults, NHANES 2003-2018  
**Exposure:** Probiotic consumption (yogurt or supplements)  
**Finding:** Negative associations between probiotics and PFOA (β=-0.19), PFOS (β=-1.27)  
**Implication:** Potential dietary intervention to reduce PFAS body burden  
**Innovation:** First large-scale evidence of modifiable PFAS exposure through diet

### IV. Environmental Exposures and Biological Aging

#### Mesnage 2025 ⭐ COMPREHENSIVE REVIEW
**Title:** Environmental Health Is Overlooked in Longevity Research  
**Journal:** Antioxidants  
**DOI:** 10.3390/antiox14040421  
**Type:** Comprehensive review  
**Pollutants:** Air PM, heavy metals, endocrine disruptors, microplastics  
**Mechanisms:**  
  - Oxidative stress → ROS production  
  - DNA damage → proteins, lipids  
  - Telomere shortening  
  - Epigenetic age acceleration  
  - Inflammation, autophagy disruption  
**Impact:** 5-10 years healthy life lost per person  
**Relevance:** Conceptual framework for environmental aging; positions PFAS in broader context

#### Ryoo et al. 2025 - NHANES HEAVY METALS
**Title:** Lead and cadmium exposure was associated with faster epigenetic aging in adults aged 50 and older  
**Journal:** Chemosphere  
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.chemosphere.2025.144194  
**Sample:** 589 adults 50-79 years, NHANES 1999-2002  
**Metals:** Blood lead and cadmium  
**Clocks:** Horvath, Hannum, SkinBlood, PhenoAge, GrimAge, GrimAge2  
**Finding:** Both metals associated with accelerated aging across multiple clocks  
**Method Comparison:** Different clocks showed varying sensitivities  
**Implication:** NHANES framework applicable to multiple environmental exposures

#### Choi et al. 2025 - NHANES TCDD & PHENOAGE
**Title:** Epigenetics-Based Age Acceleration Associated with 2,3,7,8 TCDD Exposure in Older Americans  
**Journal:** International Journal of Molecular Sciences  
**DOI:** 10.3390/ijms26041478  
**Sample:** 589 participants 50-79 years, NHANES 1999-2002  
**Exposure:** Blood 2,3,7,8-TCDD (dioxin)  
**Clocks:** 6 DNAm clocks  
**Key Finding:** Lipid-adjusted TCDD associated with PhenoAge acceleration (β=0.73, p<0.001)  
**Specificity:** PhenoAge more sensitive than other clocks for TCDD  
**Relevance:** Demonstrates PhenoAge sensitivity to endocrine-disrupting chemicals (like PFAS)

#### Yao et al. 2026
**Title:** Long-term exposure to traffic-related air pollution is associated with epigenetic age acceleration  
**Journal:** Environmental Research  
**DOI:** 10.1016/j.envres.2025.123284  
**Design:** Longitudinal, German KORA cohort  
**Exposure:** Ultrafine particles, PM10, PM2.5, NO2  
**Outcomes:** DNAm Age, DNAmTL  
**Finding:** Air pollution associated with epigenetic age acceleration and telomere shortening  
**Innovation:** Longitudinal design; repeated measurements  
**Implication:** Environmental aging is cumulative over time

#### He et al. 2025
**Title:** The association of prenatal ambient air pollution with placental epigenetic gestational age at birth  
**Journal:** Environmental Epidemiology  
**DOI:** 10.1097/EE9.0000000000000384  
**Sample:** 103 mother-offspring pairs  
**Exposure:** NO2, O3, PM2.5, PM10 during pregnancy  
**Outcome:** Placental epigenetic gestational age (Lee's clock)  
**Finding:** Higher O3 and PM10 associated with GA deceleration  
**Critical Window:** Weeks 21-31 (mid-to-late pregnancy)  
**Relevance:** Developmental windows for environmental aging effects

#### Jeremian et al. 2024
**Title:** Signatures of epigenetic, biological and mitotic age acceleration and telomere shortening associated with arsenic-induced skin lesions  
**Journal:** Archives of Dermatological Research  
**DOI:** 10.1007/s00403-024-02923-3  
**Exposure:** Arsenic (environmental)  
**Outcomes:** Multiple aging signatures (epigenetic, biological, mitotic age; telomere length)  
**Finding:** Comprehensive acceleration across all aging biomarkers  
**Implication:** Multiple environmental toxicants affect biological aging through similar pathways

### V. Lifestyle, Populations, and Biological Aging

#### Lin et al. 2021 ⭐ LARGE POPULATION STUDY
**Title:** Lifestyle factors and genetic variants on two biological age measures: evidence from 94,443 Taiwan Biobank participants  
**Journal:** Journals of Gerontology: Series A  
**DOI:** 10.1093/gerona/glab251  
**Sample:** 94,443 Taiwan Biobank (TWB1 discovery: 25,460; TWB2 replication: 68,983)  
**Biomarkers:** PhenoAge and BioAge  
**Lifestyle Findings:**  
  - BMI: +0.177 years PhenoAge per kg/m² (p<0.001)  
  - Smoking: +1.134 years PhenoAge (p<0.001)  
  - Alcohol: +0.640 years PhenoAge (p<0.001)  
**Genetics:** 11 SNPs for PhenoAge, 4 for BioAge; GCKR rs1260326 replicated from UK Biobank  
**Implication:** Large-scale validation of modifiable risk factors

#### Gilmutdinova et al. 2026
**Title:** Changes of biological age and related biomarkers in the context of a comprehensive geroprotective intervention  
**Journal:** Cardiovascular Therapy and Prevention  
**DOI:** 10.15829/17288800-2025-4686  
**Design:** Prospective follow-up  
**Sample:** 100 adults 35-55 years  
**Intervention:** 6-month lifestyle modification (diet, physical activity, stress management)  
**Biomarker:** PhenoAge  
**Finding:** Significant PhenoAge decrease, most pronounced in high-baseline quartiles (Q4: -6.73%, p<0.001)  
**Mechanism:** Decreased CRP, increased albumin, normalized hematological parameters  
**Implication:** Biological age is modifiable; intervention efficacy greater in accelerated aging

#### Arzu et al. 2024
**Title:** Associations of epigenetic age acceleration at birth and age 12 years with adolescent cardiometabolic risk  
**Journal:** Clinical Epigenetics  
**DOI:** 10.1186/s13148-024-01779-8  
**Study:** HOME cohort (Health Outcomes and Measures of the Environment)  
**Design:** Prospective pregnancy and birth cohort  
**Finding:** Epigenetic age acceleration associated with cardiometabolic risk in adolescents  
**Life-Course:** Tracks from birth through adolescence  
**Implication:** Early-life aging biomarkers predict later health

#### Luo et al. 2019
**Title:** Epigenetic aging is accelerated in alcohol use disorder and regulated by genetic variation in APOL2  
**Journal:** Neuropsychopharmacology  
**DOI:** 10.1038/s41386-019-0500-y  
**Sample:** 331 AUD patients, 201 controls  
**Clock:** Levine DNAm PhenoAge  
**Finding:** 2.22-year age acceleration in AUD (p<0.001); remained after adjusting for confounders  
**Severity:** Greater acceleration with higher GGT, ALT, heavy drinking days  
**Genetics:** GWAS identified APOL2 rs916264 associated with EAA  
**Implication:** Substance exposure accelerates biological aging; genetic susceptibility

#### Perlstein et al. 2025
**Title:** Alcohol and aging: Next-generation epigenetic clocks predict biological age acceleration in AUD  
**Journal:** Alcohol: Clinical and Experimental Research  
**DOI:** 10.1111/acer.70020  
**Sample:** 615 individuals (372 AUD, 243 controls)  
**Clocks:** GrimAge V1, V2, CausAge, DamAge, AdaptAge  
**Finding:** GrimAge V2 outperformed V1; CausAge/DamAge/AdaptAge less consistent  
**Age Subgroups:** Associations stronger in young (<40) vs old (≥40)  
**Innovation:** Comparison of causality-enriched clocks  
**Implication:** Clock selection matters for specific exposures/outcomes

#### Brown et al. 2024
**Title:** Biological Age Acceleration and Colonic Polyps in Persons under Age 50  
**Journal:** Cancer Prevention Research  
**DOI:** 10.1158/1940-6207.CAPR-24-0317  
**Sample:** 51 patients <50 years undergoing colonoscopy  
**Clocks:** Multiple (GrimAge strongest)  
**Outcome:** Preneoplastic polyps (tubular adenomas, sessile serrated adenomas)  
**Finding:** 16% higher odds of polyps per 1 year GrimAge acceleration  
**Risk Factor:** Male sex strongest predictor  
**Implication:** Biological aging relevant to early-onset colorectal cancer epidemic

#### Izquierdo et al. 2025
**Title:** Epigenetic Aging Acceleration in Obesity Is Slowed Down by Nutritional Ketosis Following VLCKD  
**Journal:** Nutrients  
**DOI:** 10.3390/nu17061060  
**Sample:** Normal weight (20), obesity (24), obesity+VLCKD (10)  
**Clocks:** Horvath, Hannum, Levine  
**Obesity:** Accelerated aging (Hv +3.4, Hn +5.7, Lv +3.9 years)  
**VLCKD Effect:** Deceleration during ketosis (Hv -3.3, Hn -6.3, Lv -8.8 years)  
**Mechanism:** Associated with BMI, ketonemia, metabolic parameters  
**Implication:** Reversibility of obesity-related biological aging

#### DeAngelis et al. 2025
**Title:** Residential Segregation and Epigenetic Age Acceleration Among Older-Age Black and White Americans  
**Journal:** Int J Environ Res Public Health  
**DOI:** 10.3390/ijerph22060837  
**Sample:** 288 Black and White adults, ACL study Wave 6 (2019)  
**Clocks:** GrimAge, PhenoAge, SkinBloodAge, HannumAge, HorvathAge  
**Finding:** Black respondents ~3 years accelerated GrimAge vs White, regardless of residence  
**Segregation:** Education, homeownership, trauma, smoking partially explain segregated Black area acceleration  
**Disparity:** Factors don't explain acceleration in non-segregated areas  
**Implication:** Structural racism and environmental inequality impact biological aging

## Notes on Study Quality

### Methodological Strengths Across Studies:
- Representative sampling (NHANES, UK Biobank, Taiwan Biobank)
- Validated epigenetic clock algorithms
- Comprehensive covariate adjustment
- Replication across cohorts
- Longitudinal designs (where applicable)
- High-quality biomarker assays

### Common Limitations:
- Cross-sectional designs limit causal inference
- Blood-based methylation may not reflect tissue-specific aging
- Exposure assessment often single time point
- Residual confounding possible
- Selection bias in DNA methylation subsamples
- Limited diversity in some cohorts

### NHANES-Specific Strengths:
- National probability sampling
- Standardized protocols
- Quality control/assurance
- Long temporal coverage
- Rich contextual data
- Publicly available (reproducibility)

## Thematic Summary

**Strongest Evidence:**
1. PhenoAge as validated aging biomarker (Levine 2018; Kuo 2021; Ho 2023)
2. PFAS-epigenetic aging link (Khodasevich 2025; Niemiec 2023; Xu 2020, 2022)
3. PFAS-cardiometabolic effects in NHANES (Bhagavathula 2026; Bashir 2022; Wu 2023)
4. Environmental pollutants accelerate aging (Mesnage 2025; Ryoo 2025; Choi 2025)

**Mechanistic Pathways:**
- Oxidative stress and inflammation
- Lipid and metabolic disruption  
- Telomere shortening
- DNA methylation alterations
- Mitochondrial dysfunction
- Immune dysregulation

**Population Implications:**
- Ubiquitous PFAS exposure in US
- Health disparities (race, SES, geography)
- Vulnerable windows (prenatal, early life)
- Modifiable through interventions
